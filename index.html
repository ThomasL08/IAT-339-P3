P3. Style Guide

Color

In designing my portfolio website, I had a clear vision of what I wanted to achieve. My goal was to create a modern and simple design that effectively showcased my work and skills. To accomplish this, I decided to use a monochromatic color scheme. Monochromatic colors are created by using shades and tints of a single color, resulting in a cohesive and visually appealing design. These colors are timeless and elegant, making them the perfect choice for achieving my desired aesthetic. By carefully selecting the right shades and tints, I was able to create a clean and sophisticated look that effectively communicates my vision for my portfolio.

Primary Color

#000000

Using black as the main color in a design can create a modern and sophisticated aesthetic. It's versatile and timeless, evoking sophistication, elegance, and minimalism. The strategic use of black can create a sense of contrast and depth, allowing other design elements to stand out and improving the overall visual experience.

Secondary Color

#ffffff

White can enhance a design's modern and minimalist aesthetic as a secondary color. The color is classic and timeless, evoking simplicity and purity, while creating a sense of balance and harmony with the primary color. This creates a clean and polished look. Additionally, white can be used to highlight important design elements, making them more visible and easier to understand. Incorporating white as a secondary color can produce an elegant and sophisticated design with a minimalist feel.

Accent Color

#64ffda

Adding cyan as an accent color can bring energy and vibrancy to a design, creating a playful and engaging aesthetic. Its strategic use can draw attention to important design elements while maintaining balance and harmony with other colors. Cyan can be paired with darker shades for a sophisticated look or with warmer colors for a striking contrast. Overall, cyan as an accent color can create an engaging and modern design.

Secondary Color

#8892B0

Steel blue, a muted shade of blue-grey, adds depth and sophistication as an accent color in design. It creates a sense of calmness and stability while drawing attention to specific design elements. Steel blue pairs well with warm or cool colors, creating striking contrasts or a calming monochromatic effect. Overall, incorporating steel blue as an accent color creates a visually appealing and modern design with a sense of sophistication and stability.

Tertiary Color

#4b4a53

Charcoal grey, a dark shade of grey, adds depth and contrast as an accent color in design. It creates a sense of sophistication and timelessness while drawing attention to specific design elements. Charcoal grey pairs well with cool and warm colors, creating striking contrasts, or as a standalone color for a minimalist aesthetic. Overall, incorporating charcoal grey as an accent color creates a visually appealing and sophisticated design with a sense of depth and contrast.

Tertiary Color

rgba(102, 0, 255, 0.2)

A semi-transparent shade of blue-purple can add a touch of sophistication and elegance to a design. Its modern and minimalist appeal creates a sense of simplicity and clarity, while its subtle use can highlight important design elements without overwhelming the overall aesthetic. This shade is perfect for adding depth and interest to a design while maintaining a clean and polished look.
